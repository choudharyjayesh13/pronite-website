Zomaland: the food-and-music carnival touring India's cities

Part festival, part food carnival, all family-friendly fun. A guide to the multi-city Zomaland experience.

Pronite Desk19 March 20262 min readIndia

Not every festival is a late-night rave. Zomaland is a daytime carnival of food, music and entertainment that tours multiple Indian cities, offering a family-friendly, all-ages festival experience built around the country's greatest love: eating.

The concept

Zomaland combines a huge spread of food stalls from popular restaurants with live music, comedy, carnival games and entertainment across a day-long event. It's accessible, upbeat and designed for a broad audience — friends, families, couples — rather than the dedicated clubbing crowd.

Why it's clever

By marrying food (the one thing every Indian gathering revolves around) with music and entertainment, Zomaland found a format that pulls a crowd well beyond the usual festival demographic. It tours widely, bringing the experience to cities that don't always get the big music festivals.

The read

If you want festival energy without the late-night commitment — or a day out that works for a mixed group — Zomaland is a genuinely fun option, and a smart example of how India's event scene keeps broadening its appeal.
