Magnetic Fields: a boutique festival in a Rajasthan palace

Electronic music, art and a 17th-century fort under desert stars. Inside India's most atmospheric boutique festival.

Pronite Desk30 January 20262 min readRajasthan

If the mega-festivals aren't your speed, Magnetic Fields might be your perfect weekend. Held at a heritage palace-fort in rural Rajasthan, it's a boutique electronic-and-arts festival famous for its extraordinary setting, thoughtful curation and intimate scale.

The setting is the star

There's nothing quite like dancing in the courtyards and ramparts of a centuries-old Rajasthani fort, with desert skies overhead and a sound system tuned for the underground. Magnetic Fields pairs that setting with carefully chosen electronic line-ups, art installations, secret stages and a crowd that comes for the whole experience, not just the headliners.

The vibe

Boutique means intimate — smaller crowds, a strong sense of community, and a design-forward, immersive atmosphere that the big festivals can't match. It's become a favourite for the discerning end of India's scene, and its Rajasthan home gives it a magic our own Udaipur events know well.

Planning

  • It's remote — travel and accommodation are part of the adventure; plan ahead.
  • Desert nights get cold; pack layers.
  • Embrace the full experience — the art and the setting are half the point.

For atmosphere over scale, few festivals in the world compare.
