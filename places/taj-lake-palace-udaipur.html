Taj Lake Palace, Udaipur

✦Expert advice from Pronite

Pronite's verdict: the single most romantic address in India. Book a lake-view suite (not a courtyard room), arrive by the hotel's private boat at dusk, and take sundowners at the Amrit Sagar bar as the City Palace turns gold. Non-guests can experience it via a booked lunch or the legendary Jharokha dinner — reserve weeks ahead. Peak-season and wedding-season rates are eye-watering; shoulder months (Sep, Feb) are the sweet spot.

Built in 1746 as a royal pleasure palace, the Taj Lake Palace seems to float on the waters of Lake Pichola, its white marble mirrored in the lake on every side. Reached only by the hotel's own boat, it is, for many, the most romantic hotel in the world — a place of lily ponds, fountain courtyards, and suites once occupied by maharajas.

Every detail — the rose-petal welcome, the candlelit dinners on the terrace, the butler service — is engineered for a once-in-a-lifetime stay. Even if you're not staying, a booked meal here is one of Udaipur's great experiences.
